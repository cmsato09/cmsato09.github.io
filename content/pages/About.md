title: ABOUT

Hi, my name is Chris and this blog chronicles my journey in coding. I did have 
a AP computer science class learning Java as a freshman in high school, but 
did not continue past that year (Java was difficult to understand at the time). 
It wasn't until several years later that I became serious in learning 
programming again. 

### Motivation
I worked with contract programmers by giving feedback on the database interface 
and testing software I used for my job. The one thing I can't forget is a 
response from one of those programmers. I would be explain what we would need 
done and ask if it was possible and the response was always 
**"Anything is possible."** My request were always possible, but it did take 
time for a fix or I needed to give more context or diagrams to make sure they 
understood what I needed. While working with them, one thought kept coming 
back to me, *"Why can't I code? Shouldn't I be able to?"*

### Starting again
It wasn't until 2020 for me to start to learn computer programming.
The world was thrown into the COVID-19 pandemic and I was furloughed for quite 
awhile. 
I chose to learn python due to its readability and popularity in the science 
field. The basics were easier to understand the second time around without the 
Java syntax. 

### Purpose of the blog
This blog is a test to see whether I can explain a concept I learned well 
enough and also serves as a document of what I learned along the way (my 
successes and failures). It may not be popular and I'm not looking for too 
much feedback here. But, if there was someone who reads it, I hope they can 
see it is possible to learn coding or learn from the mistakes I make. Many 
people state, *"Everyone can code"* or *"Everyone should learn to code,"* 
however, it may not be for everyone (and may not be for me). Nevertheless, it 
is a great way to learn how to think.
