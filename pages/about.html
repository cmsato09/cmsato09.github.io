ABOUT

Hi, my name is Chris and this blog chronicles my journey in learning computer programming. I did have a AP computer science class learning Java as a freshman in high school, but did not continue with it (Java was difficult to understand at the time). It wasn't until several years later that I became serious in learning programming again.

Motivation

I worked with contract programmers by giving feedback on the database interface and testing software I used for my job. The one thing I can't forget is a quote from one of programmers. I would be in meetings with him and ask if the thing I was requesting possible and the response from him would be "Anything is possible." And my requests were possible, but it did take time for a fix or I needed to give more context or diagrams to make sure they understood what I needed. While working with them, one thought kept coming back to me, "Why can't I code? Shouldn't I be able to?"

Starting again

It wasn't until 2020 when the world was thrown into pandemic and I was furloughed for me to start learning computer programming. I chose to learn python due to its readability (in turn, more easily understandable) and popularity. The basics were easier to understand the second time around without the java syntax.

Purpose of the blog

This blog is a test to see whether I can explain a concept I learned well enough and also serves as a document of what I learned along the way (my successes and failures). It may not be popular and I'm not looking for too much feedback here. But, if there was someone who reads it, I hope they can see it is possible to learn coding or learn from the mistakes I make. Many people state, "Everyone can code" or "Everyone should learn to code," however, it may not be for everyone (and may not be for me). Nevertheless, it is a great way to learn how to think.
